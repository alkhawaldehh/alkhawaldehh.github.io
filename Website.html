About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at Lehigh University, advised by Dr. Eugenio Schuster. My dissertation focuses on physics-based and data-driven predictive modeling and control of plasma density in tokamak fusion reactors.

My work spans adaptive control, optimal control, reinforcement learning, and system identification — all applied to pressing challenges in fusion energy research. I have conducted experimental campaigns at major fusion facilities including DIII-D (General Atomics) and KSTAR (Korea Institute of Fusion Energy).

I completed my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering at Lehigh in 2021, graduating with minors in Energy Engineering and Business Management, and was admitted into the prestigious Lehigh President's Scholar Program.

Research Interests
Adaptive Control Model Predictive Control Optimal Control (LQR) Robust Control (H∞) Reinforcement Learning Safe RL Koopman Operator Theory System Identification Neural-Network Modeling Physics-Informed ML Tokamak Plasma Control Fusion Energy

My research addresses the challenge of regulating plasma density in next-generation tokamaks such as ITER and beyond. I develop controllers that integrate physics-based transport models with machine learning techniques, enabling robust real-time control under highly uncertain reactor conditions — a critical step toward sustained thermonuclear fusion.

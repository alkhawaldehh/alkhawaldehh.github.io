About
Biography

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at Lehigh University, advised by Dr. Eugenio Schuster, with 5+ years of hands-on experience designing, implementing, and validating real-time control systems for high-performance physics experiments. My dissertation focuses on physics-based and data-driven predictive modeling and control of plasma density in tokamak fusion reactors.

My work spans adaptive control, optimal control, reinforcement learning, and system identification. I have deployed and debugged feedback controllers on live experimental devices such as DIII-D (General Atomics, San Diego) and KSTAR (Korea Institute of Fusion Energy, Daejeon), collaborating closely with cross-disciplinary teams throughout.

Prior to my Ph.D. Career, I received my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Lehigh in 2021, with minors in Energy Engineering and Business Management, and was admitted into the Lehigh President's Scholar Program.
